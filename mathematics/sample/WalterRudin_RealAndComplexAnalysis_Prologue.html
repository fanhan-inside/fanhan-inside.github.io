Theorem

  1. For every complex z we have ex ≠ 0.
  2. exp is its own derivative: exp′(z) = exp(z).
  3. The restriction of exp to the real axis is a monotonically increasing positive function, and ex→∞ as x→∞, ex→0 as x→-∞.
  4. There exists a positive number π such that eπi/2 = i and such that ez = 1 if and only if z/(2πi) is an integer.
  5. exp is a periodic function, with period 2πi.
  6. The mapping t→eit maps the real axis onto the unit circle.
  7. If w is a complex number and w ≠ 0, then w = ez for some z.
